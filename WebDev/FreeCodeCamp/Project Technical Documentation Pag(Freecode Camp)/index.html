Introduction

League of Legends (LoL) is a 5v5 online multiplayer battle arena game developed by Riot Games. Players control powerful characters known as champions and battle to destroy the enemy Nexus, the core building in their base. With a vast roster of champions and deep strategic gameplay, LoL has become one of the most popular competitive games in the world.

Getting Started

To play League of Legends:

  1. Download the game from the official Riot Games website.
  2. Create a Riot account.
  3. Complete the tutorial to understand basic controls.
  4. Start with bot matches before entering PvP.

Champions

Each champion has:

  • 1 Passive Ability
  • 3 Basic Abilities (Q, W, E)
  • 1 Ultimate Ability (R)

Roles Explained

Each player usually fills one of five roles:

  • Top Lane: Usually tanks or fighters.
  • Jungle: Roams between lanes, clears camps, and supports other lanes.
  • Mid Lane: Often mages or assassins.
  • Bot Lane: (ADC): High-damage range champions.
  • Support: Protects the ADC and controls vision.

Maps

Main map: Summoner’s Rift

  • 3 lanes: Top, Mid, Bot
  • Jungle between lanes
  • Bases at each corner
  • Baron and Dragon pits for major objectives

Other maps:

  • ARAM (Howling Abyss)
  • Teamfight Tactics (separate game mode)

Objectives

Objectives are key to winning the game and give your team powerful advantages. Securing them at the right time can shift momentum and open up opportunities.

Important team objectives:

  • Turrets: Defend lanes. Destroying gives gold and lane control.
  • Dragons: Provide team-wide buffs. 7 elemental types and Elder Dragon.
    • Chemtech Drake
    • Cloud Drake
    • Hextech Drake
    • Infernal Drake
    • Mountain Drake
    • Ocean Drake
    • Elder Dragon
  • Vouid Grubs: Small monsters that replace early Rift Herald. Killing them spawns voidlings to assist in turret sieging.
  • Rift Herald: Helps push lanes by damaging enemy structures.
  • Baron Nashor: Grants buff to empower minions and recall.

Game Phases

A Game Phase is a part of the match that reflects how the game changes over time. League of Legends matches are usually divided into three phases: Early Game, Mid Game, and Late Game — each with different goals, strategies, and priorities.

  • Early Game (0–15 mins): Focus on farming, laning, and securing early objectives like Dragons or Void Grubs. Vision control and safe play are key.
  • Mid Game (15–25 mins): Teams start grouping, pushing lanes, and fighting over larger objectives like Rift Herald or Baron. Map movement and teamwork become more important.
  • Late Game (25+ mins): One mistake can end the game. Death timers are long, and fights around Elder Dragon and Baron are often decisive. Team coordination and positioning are critical.

Runes and Items

Runes

Runes are customizable bonuses that improve your champion’s playstyle. You choose a primary path (like Precision or Sorcery) and a secondary path to fit how you want to play.

Main rune paths:

  • Precision – For sustained damage (e.g., ADCs)
  • Domination – For burst and aggressive play (e.g., assassins)
  • Sorcery – For magic damage and utility (e.g., mages)
  • Resolve – For defense and healing (e.g., tanks)
  • Inspiration – For creative and utility setups (e.g., supports)

Items

Items give you stats and effects that help in fights. As of Season 14, Mythic Items are removed, so now you can freely build based on your needs — no more 1-mythic limit.

Types of items:

  • Starter Items – For the early game (like Doran’s Blade)
  • Core Items – Give strong effects that match your champion’s role
  • Situational Items – Chosen based on the game (like anti-heal or armor)

Ranked System

Ranked is a competitive mode where players are matched based on skill. Winning games earns LP (League Points), and losing games makes you lose LP.

The ranks (from lowest to highest) are:

  1. Iron
  2. Bronze
  3. Silver
  4. Gold
  5. Platinum
  6. Emerald
  7. Diamond
  8. Master
  9. Grandmaster
  10. Challenger

Each rank (except Master and above) has Divisions (e.g., Gold IV to Gold I).

Tips for New Players

  1. Focus on 1–2 champions per role

    Learning a few champions deeply is better than knowing many poorly.

  2. Always check the minimap

    Awareness wins games. Ping missing enemies and danger zones.

  3. Use wards often

    Vision helps you avoid ganks and spot objectives. Don’t rely only on your support.

  4. Don’t chase kills, play for objectives

    Towers, dragons, and baron win games — not flashy kills.

  5. Farm consistently

    CS (creep score) gives gold. Try to hit at least 6 minions per minute.

  6. Mute toxic players early

    Staying calm and focused is more important than arguing.

    Type /mute all to avoid toxic chat.

  7. Watch replays or pro play

    Learn macro, movement, and teamfighting from better players.

  8. Play with friends if possible

    Communication and coordination are easier — and it’s more fun.
